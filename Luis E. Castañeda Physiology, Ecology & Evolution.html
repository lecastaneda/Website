Luis E. Castañeda, PhD.
 

Assistant Professor. Program of Human Genetics – Institute of Biomedical Sciences
    Faculty of Medicine – University of Chile.

Vice-president of the Sociedad de Evolución de Chile (2020–2022).
Reviewing Editor of Journal of Evolutionary Biology (2019 - present).

Career 
BSc Biological Sciences, Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile (1999 – 2003).
PhD Systematic and Ecology, Universidad Austral de Chile (2004 – 2008).
FONDECYT postdoctoral researcher, Universidad Austral de Chile (2008 – 2010).
Juan de la Cierva postdoctoral fellow, Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona, Spain (2010 – 2013).
Postdoctoral researcher, Instituto de Ecología y Biodiversidad, Chile (2013 – 2015).
Associate researcher, Universidad Austral de Chile (2016 - 2018).
Young researcher, Center of Molecular Ecology and Evolutionary Applications in Agroecosystems (2017).

Research keywords
Evolutionary physiology: evolution of stress resistance - thermal physiology - evolution and climate change.
Evolutionary quantitative genetics: evolutionary potential and constraints in native and introduced species.
Insect-plant interaction: costs of resistance mechanisms in herbivorous insects.
Metabarcoding: microbial diversity - microbial community ecology - Interactions microbiome-insect-natural enemies.

Research interests
Our lab is interested in the study of the adaptive responses of organisms to environmental stress. Specifically, we are exploring the genetic basis and phenotypic plasticity of physiological mechanisms involved in the response to different environmental stressors such as increased temperatures as a proxy of global change. We used different approaches to understand the evolution and adaptive response of animals, from ecophysiology, host-microbiome interactions, quantitative genetics to experimental evolution. 
Our main study models are different Drosophila species but we're not restricted to flies and we have several collaborations in which we explore these research topics in terrestrial insects, freshwater invertebrates, and migratory birds.

Research topics
1) Evolutionary potential and constraints of stress adaptation.
2) Genetic bases of stress adaptation.
2) Phenotypic plasticity of physiological mechanisms involved in stress resistance.
3) Microbiota and its role in plastic responses to stressful environments
4) Rapid adaptation of invasive species.
